# Fact base — Vehicle controls & K53 yard test

Sources: [Dept of Transport K53 practical test (light motor vehicles)](https://www.arrivealive.co.za/documents/k53/k53%20light%20motor%20vehicles%20code%20a%20part%201.pdf); [k53sim yard-test guide](https://k53sim.co.za/k53-yard-test-manoeuvres-parallel-parking-alley-docking-incline-start/); k53.elidge.com yard-test guides.

## Yard test (drivers-side content; enumerable)
- Manoeuvres: turn in the road (3-point), alley docking (left & right), parallel parking, incline start.
- Whole yard test (pre-trip inspection + manoeuvres) time limit: 20 min 59 s.
- Parallel parking: limited forward/reverse corrections; touching poles/lines = fail item; finish inside bay.
- Incline start: no rolling back; observation sequence before every movement (mirrors + blind spots) — examiner scores head checks.
- Alley docking: reverse into marked bay, finish fully inside, no pole/line contact.

## Pre-trip inspection (controls questions)
- Exterior: tyres, leaks, lights, direction indicators, plates; Interior: seat/mirrors/seatbelt adjust, doors closed, handbrake up, neutral before start.

## Sprint 5 verified additions (K53 practical structure + driving technique)
- **Two parts of the K53 practical test**: the yard test and the road test. Both must be passed.
- **Yard immediate-fail items** (common): rolling back on the incline start, striking a pole/kerb/line, and not observing (missing the blind-spot/head checks) before a movement.
- **Observation ("look") before every movement** in the yard and on the road: mirror(s) + blind-spot glance before moving off, changing direction, or reversing — the examiner watches your head.
- **Steering technique (K53)**: pull–push method, hands roughly quarter-to-three, feed the wheel — don't cross your arms or let the wheel spin back on its own; keep at least one hand on the wheel.
- **Cockpit drill / pre-drive**: doors closed, seat, head restraint, mirrors, seatbelt; then handbrake up, gear in neutral before starting.
- **Pre-trip EXTERIOR** (walk-around): tyres (condition + pressure), no fluid leaks under the vehicle, lights and indicators working, number plates clean/present, windscreen and mirrors clean.
- **Moving off**: neutral→start, clutch in, first gear, signal, full observation (mirror + blind spot), release handbrake, pull away smoothly at the biting point.
- **Reverse**: select only when stationary; observe over your shoulder through the rear window (not just mirrors); reverse slowly and only as far as needed.
- **Clutch discipline**: don't ride/rest your foot on the clutch (accelerates wear, reduces control); foot off the clutch except when changing gear or pulling away/stopping.
- **Braking**: progressive squeeze, look well ahead, brake in good time — don't stamp; ABS lets you steer while braking hard.
- **Stalling**: handbrake up, gear to neutral, restart, then move off with the full procedure — don't panic-restart in gear.
- **Correct gear for speed**: match the gear to the road speed; don't labour the engine in too high a gear or over-rev in too low.
- **Cover the brake** (foot poised over the brake) when approaching hazards, crossings, and where children/pedestrians may appear.

## Controls-extra batch (operation, cockpit routine, control faults)

Added to lift the controls pool for every licence code — the mock draws 8 controls
questions per paper and code 08 had only 45 to draw on. All items universal (no `codes`).

- **Driving position**: seat set so any pedal reaches the floor with a slight bend left in the knee; seatbelt fastened after the seat is set, before moving off; all doors closed and secure before moving.
- **Mirror aim**: interior mirror fills with the rear window; door mirrors show mostly the adjacent lane with a sliver of own bodywork as reference. **Blind spot** = the area mirrors don't cover, hence the physical head check.
- **Warning lights**: brake-system light on with the handbrake released points at the braking system (don't drive); engine-management light = a fault detected by the engine's control system (steady = get it read, flashing = stop when safe); ABS pedal pulsation under hard braking is normal — maintain pressure and steer.
- **Clutch & gears**: clutch down before engine speed falls to a stall when stopping; eyes stay on the road during a gear change; handbrake + neutral rather than holding the clutch at an extended stop; reverse controlled by slipping the clutch at around walking pace.
- **Coasting** in neutral or on the clutch down a hill loses engine braking and control — stay in an appropriate gear.
- **Moving off order**: clutch in → first gear → signal → full observation → release handbrake → pull away at the biting point. A signal announces intent; it does not confer right of way, and the observation still decides.
- **Steering**: pull–push, arms uncrossed — crossed arms limit further steering input and sit in the airbag's path.
- **Manoeuvres**: turn-in-the-road tests directional reversal under control and observation in a confined space (not a fixed movement count); parallel parking finishes fully inside the bay with no pole/line contact; whole yard test (inspection + manoeuvres) timed at **20 min 59 s**; observation before reversing goes all round, finishing in the direction of travel through the rear window.
- **Pre-trip extras**: brake lights need a helper or a reflective surface to verify; wiper blades must clear without smearing and washer fluid must be present.
- **Leaving the vehicle**: handbrake firmly on, engine off, key taken. Parking on a steep slope adds a gear (or Park) as a second line of defence.
- **Automatics**: select Park/Reverse only when fully stationary on the footbrake; the car creeps in Drive at idle, so keep the footbrake applied while stopped.
- **Control faults**: front tyre burst — grip firmly, ease off, slow gradually before leaving the road (no hard braking or sharp steering); brake fade from riding the brakes on a long descent; stuck accelerator — clutch in or neutral, brake, steer to a safe stop (don't kill the ignition at speed); wet brakes dried with light pressure at low speed; sudden heavy steering with the engine running = power-steering failure, two hands and stop safely.

_Not covered here on purpose:_ items turning on a regulated threshold (minimum tread depth,
equipment that must legally be carried). Those belong with the rules pack against a cited
regulation, per the roadmap's note to verify regulated facts before publishing.
