# Fact base — Sprint 7: motorcycle (A/A1) & heavy (10/14) depth

Sources: K53 motorcycle & heavy-vehicle practical manuals; National Road Traffic Act/Regulations; standard SA rider/heavy-vehicle training. Code gating: bike items `codes: ["A"]` (motorcycle group covers A/A1); heavy items `codes: ["10","14"]` general, `["14"]` articulated-specific. All fresh angles — existing coded prompts were checked to avoid duplicates.

## Motorcycle (A / A1)
- **Countersteering**: above walking pace you steer by pushing the bar on the side you want to lean toward (push left → lean/go left).
- **Road positioning**: ride in a dominant/buffer position within the lane; avoid the oily centre strip and keep space from the kerb and parked cars.
- **Cornering**: slow–look–lean–roll — set entry speed before the bend, look THROUGH the corner to where you want to go, then roll on the throttle out.
- **Braking**: use BOTH brakes; more front brake in the dry (it does most of the stopping), gentler front on loose/wet surfaces.
- **Conspicuity (SMIDSY — "sorry mate, I didn't see you")**: assume drivers haven't seen you; bright/reflective gear, headlamp on, position for visibility.
- **Loose/gravel surface**: smooth inputs, off the front brake, stay relaxed and upright.
- **Rain**: the first minutes are slipperiest; smooth throttle, brakes and steering, bigger gaps.
- **Pre-ride check**: tyres (condition + pressure), controls (brakes/throttle/clutch), lights & indicators, oil/fluids, chain tension & lubrication.
- **At a stop**: stay in gear with a foot down, and watch your mirror for a vehicle failing to stop behind you (keep an escape gap).
- **Look where you want to go**: your bike follows your eyes — target the escape path, not the hazard.
- **Truck wind blast**: expect a shove of turbulence when a large vehicle passes or you pass it; grip firmly.
- **Pillion**: a passenger must hold on, keep feet on the pegs, and lean WITH the rider — not fight the lean.

## Heavy (Code 10 rigid / 14 articulated)
- **Low air-pressure warning** (buzzer/light): stop safely and do not drive — the brakes may fail as pressure drops.
- **Rollover risk**: a high centre of gravity (especially loaded/tanker) tips easily — slow right down for circles, curves and off-ramps.
- **Off-tracking / cut-in**: on a turn the trailer's wheels track INSIDE the tractor's path, so swing wide and check the trailer doesn't cut across kerbs or cyclists.
- **Coupling (5th wheel)**: the kingpin must be fully engaged and locked by the jaws, safety catch on — then confirm with a tug test before the trip.
- **Reversing an articulated combo**: use small, opposite steering inputs (turn the wheel the "wrong" way to swing the trailer), and use a banksman/get out and look.
- **Weight distribution**: load evenly; don't overload an individual axle even if within the total GVM.
- **Overloading effects**: longer braking distance, tyre and brake overheating/failure, instability — and it's an offence.
- **Dangerous goods**: correct placards/diamonds displayed and the driver holds a PrDP (D category).
- **Dual tyres**: check both of a dual set — a deflated inner tyre is easy to miss and overloads its partner.
- **Uncoupling**: apply the trailer parking brake and lower/secure the landing legs before releasing the kingpin and pulling the tractor away.
- **Brake fade**: continuous service braking on a long descent overheats the brakes until they fade — use engine braking / the retarder and a low gear instead.
