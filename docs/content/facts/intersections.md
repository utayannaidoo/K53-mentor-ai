# Fact base — Intersections & right of way

Sources: National Road Traffic Act 93/1996 + Regulations; SA Learner Driver Manual (rules of the road / intersections); K53 defensive-driving system; [Arrive Alive rules](https://www.arrivealive.mobi/rules-on-the-road). Facts here are the stable, universally-taught SA junction rules; re-verify any numeric detail before marketing use.

## Priority rules
- **Right of way is GIVEN, never taken** — the defensive core: yield even when the rule says you have priority if the other driver hasn't.
- **Stop street (stop sign)**: full stop behind the line every time, even if clearly empty; then proceed when safe.
- **Yield sign**: give way; you may roll through without stopping if the way is genuinely clear.
- **Uncontrolled intersection** (no signs/robots/markings): the first vehicle to arrive proceeds first; if two arrive together, give way to the vehicle on your RIGHT.
- **Minor road meets major road / T-junction**: the vehicle on the terminating (minor) road yields to through traffic.
- **Vehicle already in a traffic circle** has priority over one entering.

## Robots (traffic signals)
- **Stale green** (green a long time as you approach): anticipate the change, cover the brake, be ready to stop.
- **Turning right with no green filter arrow**: you may move forward into the intersection and complete the turn when a safe gap appears — even as the light changes to amber/red, clearing the box.
- **Green does not override yielding**: turning across pedestrians or oncoming traffic still requires giving way.

## Turning
- **Turning left**: yield to pedestrians (and cyclists) crossing the road you are entering; check the left mirror/blind spot for cyclists.
- **Turning right**: yield to oncoming traffic going straight or turning left, and to pedestrians crossing your exit road.
- **Two vehicles turning right side-by-side** into a multi-lane road: stay in your own lane throughout — don't swing wide or cut across.
- Full observation sequence before every turn: mirrors → signal (in good time) → blind spot → turn.

## Traffic circles
- Signal your intention: indicate the exit as you pass the exit before yours (left indicator to leave).
- Enter only into a safe gap; never stop inside the circle.
- Multi-lane circles: left lane for the first/left exits and straight; right lane for later/right exits per markings.

## Merging
- **Zip merge** (two lanes into one): merge alternately, one from each lane, in good time — don't race to the front or block.

## Pedestrians / vulnerable users
- Pedestrians already crossing at or near a junction have right of way over turning vehicles.
- At a blind or obscured intersection: creep forward slowly until you can see, then proceed.
