# Fact base — Sprint 6 top-ups (hazard, parking, following distance)

Sources: K53 defensive-driving system; National Road Traffic Act/Regulations; SA Learner Driver Manual; [Arrive Alive road safety](https://www.arrivealive.mobi/). Stable, universally-taught SA facts; the earlier packs already cover the basics, so these are the remaining fresh angles.

## Hazard awareness (new angles)
- **Scan far ahead** (~12 seconds up the road), not fixating on the vehicle directly ahead — spot developing hazards early.
- **Space cushion / escape route**: keep space around the vehicle and always know where you'd go if something went wrong.
- **Crosswind / gusts**: high-sided vehicles and open bridges — expect a sideways push, especially when passing or emerging from shelter; hold the wheel firmly.
- **Smoke across the road** (veld fire): treat like fog — slow right down, lights on, be ready to stop; don't drive blind into it.
- **Stray/wandering animals** on rural roads (day or night): slow, be ready to stop; don't swerve violently.
- **Aggressive drivers / road rage**: don't retaliate or make eye contact; let them past, keep your distance.
- **Medication drowsiness**: some medicines impair like alcohol — don't drive if the label warns against it.
- **Stationary bus/taxi**: passengers (often children) may dart across in front of or behind it — cover the brake, pass slowly.
- **Object/debris in the road**: check mirrors before swerving; braking in a straight line is often safer than a sudden swerve.

## Parking (new angles)
- **Hill parking WITHOUT a kerb**: turn the front wheels TOWARDS the road edge (so a rolling car heads off the road, not into traffic), both uphill and downhill.
- **Securing on a steep hill**: handbrake firmly on AND leave the car in gear (1st uphill / reverse downhill for manual; P for automatic).
- **Leaving a parallel bay**: full observation — mirrors, signal, and a blind-spot/shoulder check for passing traffic and cyclists before pulling out.
- **Getting out of the car**: check the mirror and over your shoulder for cyclists/traffic before opening the door ("dooring" is a real hazard and an offence).
- **Pavement/sidewalk parking**: prohibited — it obstructs pedestrians.
- **Near a corner/bend or crest**: don't park where you reduce visibility for others.
- Don't park where leaving would force others across a barrier line or into oncoming traffic.

## Following distance (new angles)
- **Towing** a trailer/caravan: allow extra following distance — the combination is heavier and slower to stop.
- **Following an erratic/learner/unpredictable driver**: increase your gap; you need more time to react.
- **Worn tyres/brakes or a heavily loaded car**: stopping distance grows — leave more gap.
- **Space to the sides too**: don't box yourself in beside other vehicles; keep a buffer all round.
- **Why the 2-second rule beats fixed metres**: it scales automatically with speed — the faster you go, the bigger the gap it gives.
- **A bigger gap helps the driver behind you** as well: it lets you brake gently and early, preventing a chain-reaction rear-end.
